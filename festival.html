Festivals of India

Explore the vibrant festivals and traditions from different states of India.

Tamil Nadu

Pongal

Harvest festival with kolams, sugarcane, and gratitude to the Sun God.

Pongal

Thaipusam

Kavadi rituals honoring Lord Murugan with piercing and devotion.

Thaipusam

Chithirai

Grand Madurai festival celebrating Meenakshi's celestial wedding.

Chithirai

Traditions

  • Festival: Pongal is the main harvest festival, celebrated with cooking of rice and sugarcane.
  • Dance: Bharatanatyam is a classical dance form originating here.
  • Temple Culture: Rich Dravidian temple architecture (e.g., Meenakshi Temple).
  • Clothing: Men wear veshti (dhoti), and women wear sarees.

Kerala

Onam

10-day harvest festival with Pookalam, Vallam Kali, and Onasadya.

Onam

Vishu

New Year celebration with Vishukkani and family feasts.

Vishu

Thrissur Pooram

Grand temple festival held in Thrissur in April-May. Famous for decorated elephants, percussion music (chenda melam), and fireworks.

Thrissur Pooram

Traditions

  • Festival: Onam is the biggest festival, marked by boat races and floral carpets (Pookalam).
  • Dance: Kathakali and Mohiniyattam are famous dance forms.
  • Backwaters: Known for houseboat rides and scenic backwaters.
  • Clothing: Traditional white kasavu sarees and mundu for men.

Gujarat

Navratri

Nine nights of Garba and Dandiya Raas dances in vibrant attire.

Navratri

Uttarayan

Kite festival celebrated with music, food, and colorful skies.

Uttarayan

Janmashtami

Celebrates Lord Krishna's birth, especially grand in Dwarka (his legendary kingdom). Includes Ras Leela, Bhajans, and temple decorations.

Janmashtami

Traditions

  • Festival: Navratri with Garba and Dandiya Raas dances.
  • Clothing: Colorful chaniya choli (women) and kediyu (men).
  • Crafts: Famous for Bandhani (tie-dye), mirror work, and embroidery.
  • Cuisine: Known for dhokla, thepla, khakhra, and farsan (snacks)

Rajasthan

Teej

Monsoon festival with swings, songs, and traditional attire.

Teej

Gangaur

Celebration of marital bliss with processions and rituals.

Gangaur

Desert

Held in February in the Thar Desert, Jaisalmer. Showcases Rajasthani culture with folk dances, puppet shows, camel races, and turban tying competitions.

Desert

Traditions

  • Festival: Pushkar Fair, Teej, and Desert Festival are popular.
  • Dance: Ghoomar and Kalbeliya are traditional dances
  • Clothing: Bright ghagras, odhnis, and turbans with mirror work
  • Cuisine: Dal baati churma, gatte ki sabzi, and spicy snacks.

Punjab

Baisakhi

Harvest festival with Bhangra, Gidda, and community feasts.

Baisakhi

Gurpurab

Celebration of Guru Nanak's birth with prayers and processions.

Gurpurab

Lohri

Celebrated on January 13, especially in North India. Marks the end of winter and the harvesting of sugarcane. Celebrated with bonfires, folk songs, dance, and foods like rewari, gajak, and peanuts.

Lohri

Traditions

  • Festival: Baisakhi is the harvest festival; also celebrates Lohri with bonfires.
  • Dance: Bhangra and Gidda are energetic and popular.
  • Clothing: Salwar kameez with phulkari embroidery, turbans for men.
  • Cuisine: Makki di roti and sarson da saag, lassi, butter chicken.

Maharashtra

Ganesh Chaturthi

Celebration of Lord Ganesha with grand processions and eco-friendly idols.

Ganesh Chaturthi

Diwali

Festival of lights celebrated with diyas, rangoli, and sweets.

Diwali

Gudi Padwa

Marathi New Year, celebrated in March-April. People raise a Gudi (decorated flag) outside homes to symbolize victory and prosperity. Special dishes like Puran Poli are made.

Gudi Padwa

Traditions

  • Festival: Ganesh Chaturthi is the biggest state-wide celebration.
  • Dance: Lavani and Tamasha are traditional dance forms.
  • Clothing: Nauvari sarees for women, dhoti-kurta and pheta for men.
  • Cuisine: Vada pav, pav bhaji, puran poli, poha.

West Bengal

Durga Puja:

5-day grand celebration with pandals, dance, and rituals.

Durga Puja:

Poila Baisakh:

Bengali New Year celebrated with traditional sweets and cultural events.

Poila Baisakh:

Kali Puja:

Celebrated during Diwali (October-November). Worship of Goddess Kali, especially in Kolkata and Dakshineswar. Homes and temples are lit with diyas and candles.

Kali Puja

Traditions

  • Festival: Durga Puja is the most grandly celebrated festival.
  • Dance & Music: Rabindra Sangeet, Baul folk music, Chhau dance.
  • Clothing: Bengali sarees (e.g., tant, Baluchari), dhoti-kurta for men.
  • Cuisine: Fish curry, sweets like rasgulla and sandesh.
